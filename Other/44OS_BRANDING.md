# Brand Guide

Updated July 21, 2026

Welcome. This guide is here to make your work easier. It explains the few shared details that keep **forty four** and **44OS** recognizable wherever they appear.

You were invited to help because we trust your judgment and your point of view. Bring your own ideas, write in your own voice, and make work that feels natural to you. The names, logos, and basic identity are the steady pieces. Everything around them leaves room for you to create.

If something in this guide is unclear or gets in the way of good work, bring it up. This is a living reference, and the people using it should help make it better.

## forty four and 44OS

**forty four** is the parent creative company. **44OS** is its first software product.

**forty four** runs a platform and also creates original work. Engineers, artists, writers, musicians, publishers, and marketers can work together across disciplines. Over time, that may include games, films, books, original music, and other creative projects.

**44OS** is the platform where independent creative work can be published, discovered, collected, discussed, and supported. It is one product made by **forty four**, in the same way a company and its software have separate names and identities.

Future projects are directions we are exploring. They become announced products only when **forty four** shares them publicly.

## How We Work

**forty four** is built around small teams, direct communication, and people taking ownership of the work in front of them.

- Share ideas early and ask questions freely.
- Work across disciplines when another perspective can improve the result.
- Make decisions close to the work whenever possible.
- Treat experiments and revisions as part of making something good.
- Ask for help whenever context, rights, privacy, safety, or a public commitment is unclear.

Creative freedom is the starting point. Shared names and assets help the work feel connected, while each person brings the personality that makes it worth seeing.

## 44OS

44OS is a platform for independent creative work.

- **Store** helps people discover music, books, merch, and creative assets. Music can be streamed for free, and Creators can also offer paid downloads and physical products.
- **Library** keeps saved and purchased work together across signed-in devices.
- **Community** gives members and Creators a place to talk, share work, ask questions, and find collaborators.
- **Radio** plays music from the 44OS catalog through a Creator-balanced rotation.
- **Studio** gives Creators one place to publish work and updates.
- **Payments** let eligible Creators set prices and receive earnings through the supported payout process.
- **Achievements** offer new ways to explore releases and follow the work around them.

The addresses have separate jobs:

- `44os.com` is the editorial and product site for 44OS.
- `app.44os.com` is the 44OS web application.
- Desktop and mobile applications will also be 44OS products when they are released.
- A future parent-company site will represent **forty four** itself.

## Names

Two names are used throughout our work:

- **forty four** is the company name. It is always written in lowercase words.
- **44OS** is the software product. The number and letters stay together, with `OS` capitalized.

The numeric `44` can appear as the approved logo mark, but the written company name is **forty four**.

Other forms such as `44`, `Forty Four`, or `FORTY FOUR` are not used as the written company name. Forms such as `44 OS` and `44os` are not used for the product. Keeping these two names consistent is the most important purpose of this guide.

## Writing in Your Voice

There is no house caption formula and no outreach script. Speak like yourself. A post can be direct, funny, curious, thoughtful, minimal, or expressive when that voice fits the work and treats the people involved with respect.

Clear language usually works best. Name the Creator and the work accurately, say what is happening, and give people a useful reason to look or listen. Let the Creator's work remain the focus.

These are simple starting points, not templates:

- `MUSES by TELLALI is now available on 44OS.`
- `Listen to the release on 44OS.`
- `We asked the Creator about the story behind the work.`
- `I am part of the forty four team and wanted to reach out about your work.`

Feel free to rewrite, expand, or take a completely different direction. Accuracy matters more than sounding like everyone else. Public claims about an unreleased product, partnership, pricing change, payout policy, or legal matter are good moments to check in with our team first.

## Logo System

**forty four** and **44OS** share one simple two-color mark:

- The white mark is available for dark backgrounds.
- The black mark is available for light backgrounds.
- Social profile images use the white mark on black.

The approved files preserve the shape and proportions of the mark. A little space around it helps it remain clear. As a useful starting point, leave space equal to about one quarter of the mark's height on every side, and keep it at least 32px wide on screen or 10mm wide in print.

Variations such as stretching, cropping, outlining, recoloring, or rebuilding the mark with typed characters make it harder to recognize. Starting from an approved asset is the easiest way to keep it consistent.

## Social Media and Marketing

The identity is intentionally simple so the Creator and their work can lead. Use the correct name and logo, then make the rest of the composition your own.

### Typography

Inter is the shared typeface for social graphics and internal marketing materials. It is included in the Brand Kit and can be used in Sketch and other design software.

For a 1080px-wide canvas, this scale is a useful starting point:

- Headline: around 96px, weight 700.
- Subheading: around 48px, weight 600.
- Body: around 38px, weight 400.
- Metadata: around 28px, weight 500.

These sizes are suggestions, not a fixed template. Adjust them for the artwork, amount of copy, and format. Exact Creator names and release titles keep the capitalization used in 44OS.

### Instagram Canvas Guide

These are Instagram's common working sizes:

- Portrait feed post: 1080 by 1350, 4:5.
- Square feed post: 1080 by 1080, 1:1.
- Story or Reel: 1080 by 1920, 9:16.

For a comfortable starting layout, try keeping important elements at least 80px from a feed edge. On Stories and Reels, around 90px on the left and right and 250px at the top and bottom leaves room for interface controls.

Published artwork and the exact display names in 44OS are reliable source material. Private messages, email addresses, payment details, drafts, and unpublished work stay private unless the person involved has explicitly approved their use.

## Working With People

Team members have room to create posts, talk with Creators, and reach out to new people in their own way. Be honest about being part of the **forty four** team, treat people with respect, and avoid promising a result that has not been decided yet.

Most everyday posts and conversations do not need approval. Ask our team to review a message when it speaks for the whole company or concerns legal matters, payout policy, pricing policy, unreleased products, partnerships, safety, or a new identity. That check-in protects everyone while leaving routine creative work in your hands.

## For Developers

The product and editorial sites have a broader interface system than social materials. This section is a quick orientation for anyone building a new **forty four** or **44OS** surface.

### Accent Colors

44OS supports four interface accents. Ocean is the current default, while people can choose another accent in Settings:

- Ocean: `#60A5FA`
- Amber: `#F59E0B`
- Sage: `#7CFF4F`
- Violet: `#B56CFF`

Accent color helps communicate focus, selection, and state. It belongs to the application interface and does not recolor the black-and-white logo.

### Materials

The application uses four material roles:

- **Environment** is the background behind the application window.
- **Shell glass** is the unified surface behind the Dock, Topbar, and workspace.
- **Glass** is the flat material for ordinary panels, controls, inputs, and lists.
- **Paper** is the raised material for menus, dropdowns, popovers, and selection lists.

Glass stays flat, with a hairline border and no independent shadow. Paper and clickable media cards use the shared raised shadow so they read as objects above the workspace.

### Corners, Borders, and Elevation

The primary radius steps are 14px, 18px, 22px, 26px, and 34px. Pills and avatars use a fully rounded radius. Most panels use 24px on desktop and 16px on mobile. Inputs use 16px on desktop and 12px on mobile.

Borders are usually one-pixel semantic hairlines: black at 16% on light surfaces and white at 14% on dark surfaces. The system has only two content elevation states:

- **Flat** uses no shadow.
- **Raised** uses `0 18px 48px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.16)` in light mode and `0 20px 54px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.38)` in dark mode.

The application shell has its own deeper window shadow and remains separate from content elevation.

### Application Typography

44OS uses the operating system's native sans-serif stack. A compact set of roles carries most screens:

- Page title: 26px on a 32px line, weight 700.
- Section title: 22px on a 26px line, weight 700.
- Card title: 13px on a 16px line, weight 600.
- Body: 13px on an 18px line, weight 400.
- Supporting text: 12px on a 15px line, weight 500.
- Metadata: 10px on a 13px line, weight 400.

Mobile feature displays can grow to 42px when a page needs a stronger opening statement. The semantic roles above should be reused before adding a new type treatment.

### Editorial Surfaces

The public 44OS editorial site and internal documents use a calm, high-contrast palette:

- Paper: `#F5F5F0`
- Ink: `#1A1A1A`
- Black: `#000000`
- White: `#FFFFFF`

Layouts favor generous spacing, direct hierarchy, simple pill controls, and useful product imagery. Inter can be used for internal documents and new editorial materials when appropriate.

### 44OS Application

The 44OS application keeps its established dark interface, system font stack, spacing, components, accessibility behavior, and Ocean `#60A5FA` interface accent. Ocean belongs to the application interface and is not a logo color.

Existing components and the private 44OS UI reference are the starting point for application work. New screens should feel like part of the product before introducing a new visual pattern.
