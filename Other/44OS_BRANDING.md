## Start Here

This guide explains how to represent 44 and 44OS clearly. It is a working source of truth for names, writing, identity, social posts, and outreach.

Team members have room to make decisions and create original work. That freedom operates inside a few firm boundaries: use the approved names and artwork, protect private information, describe only what is live, and request owner approval for the sensitive subjects listed below.

Changes to this guide are reviewed in the repository. Do not keep a separate personal version. If this guide and an old file disagree, use this guide and report the conflict.

## What 44 Is

**44** is the parent creative company. **forty four** is the current lowercase operator reference. **44OS** is the first product.

44 creates platforms and original creative work. Engineers, artists, writers, musicians, publishers, and marketers work together. The company can build the tools that distribute work and the work that lives inside those tools.

Games, films, books, and original music are directions the company is building toward. They are not announced products or release promises until 44 publishes an official announcement.

The exact registered entity name, business address, DBA status, and trademark status are intentionally absent from public language until each fact is verified.

## How 44 Works

44 uses flat, multidisciplinary collaboration as an operating principle.

- People own work directly and communicate without unnecessary layers.
- Teams form around the work that needs to be done.
- Engineering, art, writing, music, publishing, support, and marketing can collaborate without artificial department walls.
- The person closest to a problem should be able to explain it and help solve it.
- Autonomy includes accountability for accuracy, privacy, legal review, and the final result.

Creative freedom does not override confidentiality, platform safety, rights ownership, or an assigned operational owner.

## 44OS

44OS is a platform for independent creative work.

- **Store** helps people discover music, books, merch, and creative assets. Music can be streamed for free. Creators can also offer paid downloads and physical products.
- **Library** keeps saved and purchased work together across signed-in devices.
- **Community** lets members and Creators talk directly, share work, ask questions, and find collaborators.
- **Radio** plays music from the 44OS catalog through a Creator-balanced rotation.
- **Studio** gives Creators one place to publish work and updates.
- **Payments** let eligible Creators set prices and receive recorded earnings through the supported payout process.
- **Achievements** give members new ways to explore releases and follow the work around them.

Use these addresses exactly:

- `44os.com` is the 44OS editorial and product site.
- `app.44os.com` is the 44OS web application.
- Desktop and mobile applications are future 44OS clients until they are publicly released.
- The future parent-company site remains unnamed until its domain is selected.

44OS is a product of 44. It is not the parent company itself.

## Names and Writing

Write the approved names exactly:

- `44`
- `forty four`
- `44OS`

Do not write `44 OS`, `44os`, `Forty Four`, or an invented abbreviation in approved company or product copy.

Use short, concrete sentences and ordinary words. Describe what something does before describing how it feels. Let the product and the Creator's work carry the message.

Avoid hype, unsupported superlatives, copied slogans, AI-like filler, and decorative em dashes. Never imply that a feature, payout route, client platform, partnership, event, or release exists before it is live.

### Approved examples

- `44OS is a platform for independent creative work.`
- `Listen to the release on 44OS.`
- `MUSES by TELLALI is now available on 44OS.`
- `I am part of the 44 team and I am reaching out about your work.`
- `Open Studio to publish music, books, merch, sample packs, and updates.`

### Corrections

- Instead of `The world's most revolutionary creator platform`, write `A platform for independent creative work.`
- Instead of `44 OS has changed everything`, write the specific feature or release that changed.
- Instead of `Available everywhere`, name the clients or countries that are currently supported.
- Instead of `You will be featured`, explain that the team is reviewing work and make no promise.

### Caption pattern

Use the exact system display name and exact published title. State what is available, where it is available, and one useful detail. Keep the 44 identity secondary to the Creator.

### Outreach pattern

Introduce yourself as part of the 44 team. Say why you are contacting the person. Ask one clear question. Do not use artificial urgency or promise placement, sales, payment, or featuring.

## Logo System

44 and 44OS use one neutral two-color logo system. Use the white 44 mark on black or another dark background. Use the black 44 mark on white, Paper, or another light background. Social profile images use the white mark on black.

Ocean `#60A5FA` is an interface accent. It is not a logo color.

Until final masters complete optical review, use these provisional limits:

- Clear space: at least one quarter of the mark's total height on every side.
- Minimum digital width: 32px.
- Minimum print width: 10mm.

Always use the approved asset file. Do not recreate the mark with typed characters.

Do not stretch, rotate, crop, outline, redraw, recolor, shadow, glow, animate, merge with another mark, or place the mark over low-contrast imagery. Do not add a trademark or registration symbol until its legal status is verified.

Logo masters in the provisional Brand Kit are review assets. They are not final production masters until the owner approves them.

## Color and Materials

The core editorial palette is intentionally small:

- Paper: `#F5F5F0`
- Ink: `#1A1A1A`
- Black: `#000000`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Ocean interface accent: `#60A5FA`

Use Ink on Paper and white on black for primary editorial layouts. Ocean can appear inside product-interface screenshots and interface diagrams.

Do not place grey text on a similarly light grey background. Do not recolor either logo master. Ocean is an interface accent, not a logo color.

## Typography

Use self-hosted Inter for Brand Guide materials, internal marketing documents, and social assets. Keep the 44OS application and the public 44OS landing page on their existing system font stacks.

Inter is distributed under the SIL Open Font License. Do not use or distribute Apple SF, Spotify Mix, or another proprietary typeface in 44 templates.

Default social scale on a 1080px-wide canvas:

- Headline: 96px, line height 0.98, weight 700, tracking `-0.02em`.
- Subheading: 48px, line height 1.15, weight 600.
- Body: 38px, line height 1.35, weight 400.
- Metadata: 28px, line height 1.25, weight 500.

Avoid tracking adjustments on body copy, excessive all-caps text, and more than three hierarchy levels in one composition. Exact display names and release titles keep their original capitalization.

## Instagram Standards

Use these canvases:

- Primary feed: 1080 by 1350, 4:5.
- Square fallback: 1080 by 1080.
- Story and Reel: 1080 by 1920, 9:16.

Keep at least 80px from every feed edge. For Stories and Reels, keep important text and marks at least 90px from the left and right edges and 250px from the top and bottom.

Use published Creator artwork and the exact display names stored in 44OS. Keep the 44 or 44OS identity secondary to the featured Creator or work.

Never expose private messages, account email addresses, payment information, drafts, unpublished material, moderation details, or private files. Editable SVG templates in the Brand Kit are designed to open in Sketch.

## Outreach and Approval

Team members may independently publish routine posts about Creators, releases, Community activity, and live platform features.

Owner approval is required before publishing:

- Legal statements or claims about company registration and trademarks.
- Payout claims, payout policy, or earnings promises.
- Pricing policy or a change to commerce terms.
- Roadmap announcements or future client release dates.
- Partnerships, sponsorships, acquisitions, or endorsements.
- Crisis responses, company statements, or safety incidents.
- A new logo, color, lockup, or identity variant.

Outreach must identify the sender as part of the 44 team. Do not spam people or promise that their work will be featured. Escalate rights, privacy, payment, threats, self-harm, or safety issues through the established Support and Admin process.
